FAQ

Is this trying to substitute universities?

No, the objective is to complement them, rather than substituting them. The system will be a tool for university professors to unite efforts.

What is the role of universities in this platform?

Universities remain the primary certifying entities. The platform proposes that universities adhere to the open-source curriculum, contribute to its development, and use the content in their classes. Students' certifications are recorded on the platform, creating a standardized and compatible certification system.

How is this different from other online learning platforms?

It stands out by offering a collaborative curriculum development system and by integrating with current universities, ensuring the recognition and compatibility of the education provided.

How can individuals contribute to the collaborative development of the curriculum?

The platform encourages individuals to make proposals for changes to units or their dependencies. Proposals undergo a democratic voting process, and individuals can actively participate by voting and providing feedback. The collaborative nature of the platform allows for a diverse range of contributions.

What about the concerns of job displacement for traditional professors?

The platform aims to enhance, not replace, traditional teaching. If the platform is trying to optimize education and reduce costs, it is not to reduce the resources/investment, but to reach further; and professors will still play a crucial role in supervising tasks, providing feedback, and contributing to the collaborative curriculum.
